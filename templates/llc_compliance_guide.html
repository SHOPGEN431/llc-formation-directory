LLC Compliance Overview

Maintaining LLC compliance is crucial for preserving your liability protection and keeping your business in good standing. Regular filings and updates ensure your LLC remains legally active.

Liability Protection

Maintaining compliance helps preserve the "corporate veil" that protects your personal assets.

Good Standing

Regular compliance keeps your LLC in good standing with state authorities.

Business Credibility

Compliance demonstrates professionalism and reliability to customers and partners.

Avoid Penalties

Timely compliance prevents late fees, penalties, and potential dissolution.

Annual Compliance Requirements

These are the most common annual requirements for LLCs across all states.

Annual Reports
What They Include:
  • Current business address
  • Registered agent information
  • Member/manager details
  • Business activities
  • Filing fee payment
Deadlines:
  • Varies by state (anniversary date)
  • Some states have specific due dates
  • Late fees apply for missed deadlines
  • Online filing available in most states
Tax Filings
Federal Requirements:
  • Personal tax return (April 15)
  • Partnership return (March 15)
  • Estimated tax payments (quarterly)
  • Self-employment tax
State Requirements:
  • State income tax returns
  • State estimated payments
  • Franchise tax (if applicable)
  • Sales tax filings

Ongoing Compliance Requirements

Maintain these requirements throughout the year to keep your LLC compliant.

Registered Agent
Requirements:
  • Maintain active registered agent
  • Update agent information
  • Ensure availability during hours
  • Accept service of process
Updates Needed:
  • Address changes
  • Agent resignation
  • Service cancellation
  • Annual renewals
Business Records
Required Records:
  • Articles of Organization
  • Operating Agreement
  • Meeting minutes
  • Financial statements
  • Tax returns
Retention:
  • Keep records organized
  • Store securely
  • Maintain for 7+ years
  • Update regularly
Licenses & Permits
Common Requirements:
  • Business licenses
  • Professional licenses
  • Sales tax permits
  • Industry-specific permits
Renewals:
  • Annual renewals
  • Continuing education
  • Fee payments
  • Compliance updates

State-Specific Requirements

Compliance requirements vary significantly by state. Check your state's specific requirements.

High-Compliance States:
Delaware
  • Annual report: $300
  • Franchise tax
  • Complex calculations
California
  • Annual report: $20
  • Franchise tax: $800
  • LLC tax: $800+
Low-Compliance States:
Wyoming
  • Annual report: $50
  • No state income tax
  • Simple requirements
Nevada
  • Annual report: $150
  • No state income tax
  • Business license: $200
